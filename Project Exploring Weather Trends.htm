Project Exploring Weather Trends by Hugo Britto
The goal of this project will be to create a visualization and prepare a write up describing the similarities and differences between global temperature trends and temperature trends in the closest big city to where I live.
OBSERVATIONS
1- The graph shows that Seattle is cooler on average compared to the average global temperature and this difference has been consistent over time. In the mobile average in the first 50 years (from 1846 to 1896), the difference between the global average (8.11) and the Seattle average (7.3) was 0.81, but in the last year analyzed (2013) the interval increased to 1.09. That means the average global temperature is rising faster than Seattle's temperature.
2- Another thing noted, Seattle's average temperature has been oscillating regarding the temperature drop than the average global temperature. For example, while the global average temperature had two years of decay, Seattle had thirty-nine years during the same period analyzed. This also explains the growth of the range between the two temperature averages over time.
3- The average temperature of the two samples has been consistently growing over the years with an average of 0.01 per year. This situation is responsible for making Seattle and the global average temperature warmer.
4- In the last twenty years (considering 2013 as the last analyzed year), the global temperature had a contribution rate of 30% (0,31) in respect to the sum of the total moving average temperature in the analyzed period. That means that the variation more than doubled from an average of 0.0064 per year in the first 92 years to 0.0155 for the last twenty years. So the general trend is that the world temperature is getting warmer and the speed that is happening this is of great concern. After finding this result, I researched why this has been happening and discovered a few reasons. 1- Manufacturing production has doubled in three departments (marketwatch.com). 2- From 1950 to 2010, global primary energy consumption quintupled, of that total 80% was supplied by fossil fuels (deepresource.wordpress.com).
